# Layout Patterns

Choose one layout pattern only after resolving intent, content, ratio, and style. Every asset has one primary focal point.

## Cover patterns

- Hero title: a short exact title with one symbolic or material visual hook.
- Hero number: one exact value with its unit as the dominant hook.
- Comparison: two clearly separated sides with one contrast statement.
- Trend: one clean direction or curve supported by the exact metric.
- Structure: a compact system map with one dominant center or path.
- Annotated object: one object with no more than five short labels.

Covers should remain legible at thumbnail scale. Prefer one hook, one supporting line, and generous safe margins.

## Illustration patterns

- Cycle: a closed sequence whose direction is unambiguous.
- Pipeline: a left-to-right or top-to-bottom ordered process.
- Hub-and-spoke: one center with clearly related satellites.
- Before/after: one controlled contrast with invariant elements.
- Layer stack: ordered layers with clear containment or dependency.
- Data scene: exact data remains dominant; scene elements are secondary.
- Scientific mechanism: causal parts and motion are explicit and accurate.
- Symbolic scene: one metaphor that clarifies an abstract or humanities concept.

For explanatory images, use at most five short labels unless the user explicitly requests more. Attach labels to the object, step, line, or region they describe.

Avoid multiple competing focal points, decorative dashboards, dense paragraphs, fake interface chrome, and unlabeled data marks.
